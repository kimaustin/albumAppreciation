From a scale from 1 - 10, how important is music in your life?

7, I listen to music very often, almost like a couple hours a day, I feel like I have a lot of playlists with a lot of different moods, so it influences my mood, and also related to what I am doing at the time. And, I'm studying a lot so I listen to it then too.

Would you say that you are a “music” head? (i.e. Metal-head, hip-hop-head, etc.) If so, what is your favorite genre of music?

I'm shaking my head, so no!

Then do you have a favorite genre of music, or style, or mood

I like alternative pop, rnb, basically I like fusion, some old such christian, no more EDM.

How has your taste in music changed over the past 10 years?

It’s gotten more mellow, I think when I first started listening to music, it was very mainstream, it was what everyone else was listening to. And then I started being all ‘soundcloud’, like looking for new things and remixes and underground artist, so I went through that phase. And then, went through an EDM and electronic music. And then I think I transition into alternative electronic, and then alternative rnb, and then more indie kinda stuff, and then classic music/jazz. So it’s gotten more mellow, and I think that reflects my sentient on life too.

life too.

Why do you think that is-that you started off listening to what just everyone else listened to, mainstream music?

I think it’s just whats accessible, because that s like the radio, that's what everyone is listening to. So your first exposure is to what is accessible, and you grab onto that, until you start to look for your own music yourself. Because when you find like underground music or music that is not as well known you have to go through the process of actually listening and finding whether or not you like that.

When listening to new music, what aspect of the music do you most pay attention to (i.e. the beat/vibe, unexpectedness, wordplay, vocal talent)?

I usually first listen to the beginning, middle, and end, and then find if its worth dumping into a playlist, where I later listen to fully, and then that's when I start to weed out. I think I listen for intonation, the mood it evokes for me, how I connect to what I'm listening to, what else… flow, singers voice. Honestly, if theres a nice melody and the lyrics don't make any sense to me, I’ll take it out.

How often are you listening to music/have headphones in on an average day?

Uh a decent amount, I usually listen to it when I wake up, I like morning music and it keeps me awake. Usually when I’m walking, I sing sometimes too. And I always listen to music when I'm studying. When I'm reading the bible I'm listening to music too. Yeah, it just depends. I don’t consider it listening if the music is just playing in the background.

Oh so you don’t consider that music?

I don't know, is it?

I don't know what do you think? Whats your reasoning for one way or the other?

I think it’s still considering listening, but theres a difference between active listening and passive listening.

Mmm. How often do you practice active listening?

Not that often to be honest… just because life is just like that. I feel like when I'm exploring music I'm passively listening. I think active listening is when I'm listening to music and that's it.

Say you're studying, do you play a playlist that is new and says will provide this kind of mood, or do you play something that you’ve already made that you know will provide the specific mood that you need?

Most of the time its from things I already know, if I listen to something new, I end up distracted because Ill just start listening to it. My ears just start to transition to that, yeah.
